### 1. RSLS Field Theory & Differential Geometry
 * **g_{\mu\nu}**: The gravitational metric tensor
defining the dynamical spacetime on the 4-dimensional
pseudo-Riemannian manifold.[1]

representing the kinematic state and causal propagation of
information.[1]

dissipative scalar field acting as an Israel-Stewart state
variable tracking local informational entropy.[1]
 * **M_{\text{max}}**: The absolute saturation limit
(thermodynamic capacity bound) of the memory field.[1]
 * **V(M)**: The strictly convex logarithmic barrier
potential (the "Holographic Wall") preventing the memory
field from exceeding its maximum limit.[1]
 * **\alpha**: The dynamic metric lapse function
representing gravitational time dilation.[1]
 * **\beta^r / \beta^\phi**: The spatial shift vector
representing radial information inflow or geometric
frame-dragging.[1]
 * **\ell_***: The emergent macroscopic relaxation
length scale, establishing the physical thickness of the

diffuse-interface phase boundary (the entropy wall).[1, 1]
 * **\tau_M**: The thermodynamic relaxation time
representing the causal lag between geometric forcing and
informational response.[1]

governing the Maxwell-Cattaneo telegrapher's equation
limit.[1]
 * **\mu**: The intrinsic stiffness modulus, utilized as a
parabolic spatial gradient penalty to enforce Bounded
Variation (BV) closure across the phase boundary.[1]
 * **J_\mu**: The dynamically evolving entropy-gradient
flux vector utilized to maintain strict Lorentz causality
(subluminal transport).[1]
 * **\theta**: The geometric expansion scalar
(\nabla_\mu U^\mu) tracking spatial volume changes.[1]
 * **\kappa**: In the kinematic sector, this acts as the
Lagrange multiplier enforcing the vector flow
normalization (U^\mu U_\mu = 1).[1] In the Tensor
Bridge, it acts as the proportionality constant derived via
least squares (\langle T \rangle = \kappa \cdot G) mapping
the field to General Relativity.[1]
 * **T_{\mu\nu}**: The spatial Martin-Siggia-Rose
(MSR) stress-energy tensor generated by the field.[1, 1]

tensor via the Poisson equation.[1]

### 2. Acoustic Sphere & Hardware Geometry
 * **R**: The internal physical radius of the acoustic
sphere cavity.[1]
 * **j_{nl}**: The exact zero (root) of the spherical
Bessel function dictating the resonant spatial mode (e.g.,
j_{01} = \pi).[1]
 * **k_{nl}**: The spatial wavenumber of the cavity
mode, exacted as j_{nl}/R.[1]
 * **f_{nl}**: The fundamental acoustic resonant
frequency of the selected mode.[1]
 * **L_{pend}**: The pendulum length, strictly derived
to synchronize its period with the sphere's acoustic
resonance.[1]
 * **r_0**: The dynamic physical radius of the Venturi
throat, constrained to sit at exact resonance with the
Bessel zeros.[1]
 * **r_{in}**: The fixed inner cutoff radius of the
Venturi, derived from the preceding Bessel zero
(j_{(n-1)l}).[1]
 * **G**: The Bernoulli gain, establishing the fluidic
injection coupling strength strictly from the ratio of r_{in}
to r_0.[1, 1]
 * **d**: The spacing of the blind gate acting as a
diffraction grating to produce a first-order optical
maximum.[1]
 * **f_{fan}**: The mechanical fan modulation
frequency.[1]
 * **T_{star}**: The exact stellar temperature (in Kelvin)

that the closed system geometry is perfectly resonant
with.[1]
